Overview

I will be creating a low poly character for a computer game. This includes the modelling the character and rigging it. We rig it so we could produce different animations for when they're idle or running. The character will have 11 parts; forearm and brachium for both sides (4), the calf and quads for both legs (4), torso, neck and head.
My specific character will be a male assasin, requiring a cloak and dagger. These are additions to my design. It will take heavy inspiration from the assasins creed games.

End Users

The target audience for these cloak designs consists of game developers specializing in action-adventure games with characters adept in stealth mechanics. These developers primarily target male gamers between the ages of 14 and 25, as this demographic represents the consumer base for such games. Understanding the preferences and expectations of their audience, these developers seek cloak designs that resonate with the visual style and narrative themes of their games. They value flexibility in design to accommodate potential shape changes or game changes, to align with evolving gameplay mechanics and aesthetic preferences of not only tjem, but the target audience aswell.







Requirements

Requirements are thing my character need to be succesful. Not only succes in design but also success in animation.

  • Legs

    • Designed for swift and silent movement, the calf and quad should feature streamlined shapes to minimize noise and friction against surfaces.
    • Shoes should have a sleek, low-profile design with textured soles for enhanced traction and stability.
    • Rigging should enable realistic flexion and extension of the legs, with special attention to the ankle joint for dynamic foot movements. 3 rigging joint (calf, quad and foot)

  • Torso

    • The torso serves as the central hub for character movement and interaction, requiring a sturdy yet flexible design to support various actions.
    • The capelet attached to the shoulders should have fluid cloth physics, allowing it to react dynamically to character movements and environmental factors.
    • A functional belt with intricately detailed buckles and pouches adds visual interest and provides storage space for essential tools or items.
    • Not rigged

  • Arms

    • Arms should be modeled with defined musculature and realistic proportions to convey strength and agility.
    • The humerus and forearm should feature smooth transitions between segments, facilitating fluid movement and posing.
    • 2 rigging joints, upper arm and forearm (connected by elbow)

  • Head

    • The cloaked head should exude an air of mystery and intrigue, with the cloak draping elegantly over the shoulders and framing the face.
    • Eyes should be expressive and lifelike, capable of conveying a wide range of emotions and reactions to make up for the character not having a mouth.
    • Neck rigging should allow for smooth rotation and tilting movements, enhancing the character's believability and expressiveness.
    • Neck is rigged

  • Cloak

    • The cloak should have a flowing silhouette with subtle texture details, conveying a sense of weight and movement as the character navigates the environment (It wont be rigged this is specific to design).
    • Designed to provide camouflage and protection, the cloak should feature muted colors and naturalistic patterns that blend seamlessly with the surroundings.

  • Rigging

    • Rigging is essential for bringing the character to life through animation, allowing for realistic movement and interaction with the game world.
    • Each component, including the head, legs, arms, and neck, should be meticulously rigged to enable seamless articulation and posing.

  • Animation

    • Animation should emphasize the character's agility and stealth, with fluid movements that reflect their swift and precise actions.
    • Running animations should convey speed and grace, with subtle secondary motions in the capelet and cloak to enhance realism.
    • Idle animations should capture the character's focused demeanor, with occasional shifts in weight and subtle gestures to maintain engagement.
